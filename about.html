. about us .

SpitzFM is a radio station operating from the Northwest England.
It is run entirely by volunteers who live and work in the area and attempts to empower local people to have a voice which might otherwise go unheard.​ We are proud 80s babies and we share a passion for music and local radio. Kikem and Charles We were 18 years old when we started falling deep into radio shows on that big old Okoli’s radio stereo in the Unity Room of Caritas University Enugu Nigeria where we enjoyed Spaceman on the microphone broadcasting from Cosmo Fm Enugu Studios and as fate would have it Kikem went on to work with spaceman as a content creator for Mtech Ltd.
I would call Spaceman my music admierer as he was a genius with the mic. We both went on to study different things I went for my Masters in Environmental Management, Oil and Gas and Charles worked for another company. Lina to the Rescue: We found it hard to find a name for our radio station and we our first-ever broadcast 2023 when we created the domain name and broadcasted on Live365. Going Live: We then went legit to launch our very own web player so the world would hear our taste in music from all over the world building the first Internation music radio station website which will serve as a proxy server providing encrypted connection to geo-fenced locations. Studio: Follow our journey: We swapped my bedroom in Manchester to get a Studio where we will make and record music content and collaborate with people. We know what the people want. We are deep like that. Problem? No solution: We're creating our own radio platform because the current landscape lacks what we believe radio should offer. With FM's decline and the effects of sanitization, nationalization, and commercialization, listeners like you are missing out. We're a different breed, so we're taking matters into our own hands." ​ Now that you know who we are, perhaps you might want to know WHY we are doing what we do.
Our mission / manifesto is to:

  • promote local services and community groups.
  • Ride on the D**ks of CosmoFm’s glory days and reminisce on good music
  • give people in the area the chance to learn social skills and gain qualifications in radio presentation /production, leading to better chances of employability, regardless of experience.
  • promote equality and bring people together through evangelism, gospel teachings– regardless of age, race, gender, sexual orientation or religious beliefs and promote opportunities to Plant more trees to reduce deforestation in Africa.
